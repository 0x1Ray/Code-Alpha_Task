üö® What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers impersonate legitimate organizations to steal sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data. These attacks typically occur through email, text messages, or fake websites.

üß† Social Engineering Tactics

Social engineering exploits human psychology rather than technical vulnerabilities. Attackers use psychological manipulation to trick people into divulging information or performing actions.

Common Psychological Triggers

üò∞

Fear

"Your account will be suspended!"

üéÅ

Real Social Engineering Scenarios

CEO Fraud: "This is your CEO. I need you to transfer $50,000 immediately for an urgent acquisition. Don't tell anyone - it's confidential."
Tech Support Scam: "Your computer is infected! Call this number immediately to prevent data loss."
Romance Scam: After building an online relationship: "I'm stuck overseas and need money for a medical emergency."

üõ°Ô∏è Psychological Defense Strategies

  • Pause and Think: Take time before responding to urgent requests
  • Verify Independently: Contact the person/organization through official channels
  • Trust Your Instincts: If something feels off, it probably is
  • Don't Let Emotions Drive Decisions: Attackers exploit fear and excitement

üîê Password & Account Security

  • Use unique passwords for each account
  • Enable two-factor authentication whenever possible
  • Use a password manager to generate and store strong passwords
  • Regularly update passwords for critical accounts
  • Monitor account activity and set up alerts

üö® What to Do If You've Been Phished

  1. Change passwords immediately on all affected accounts
  2. Contact your bank/credit card companies if financial info was compromised
  3. Run antivirus scans on your devices
  4. Report the incident to your IT department or relevant authorities
  5. Monitor accounts closely for unusual activity
  6. Consider identity monitoring services if personal data was stolen

üìß Real-World Examples

Example 1: Fake Bank Email

From: security@bank0famerica.com
Subject: URGENT: Account Suspended - Verify Now!

Dear Valued Customer,

We have detected suspicious activity on your account. Your account has been temporarily suspended for your protection.

You must verify your identity within 24 hours or you account will be permanently closed.

Click here to verify: www.bankofamerica-security.net/verify

Thank you,
Bank of America Security Team
Red Flags Identified:
  • Misspelled domain: "bank0famerica.com" (zero instead of 'o')
  • Urgent language and threats
  • Suspicious link domain
  • Generic greeting "Dear Valued Customer"
  • Grammar error: "you account"

Example 2: COVID-19 Scam

From: cdc-alerts@health-gov.org
Subject: COVID-19 Vaccine Appointment Available

Important Notice:

Due to high demand, we are offering priority vaccine appointments. You have been selected based on your demographics.

Secure your appointment now by providing your information:
- Full Name
- Social Security Number
- Insurance Information

Act fast - only 24 hours remaining!

CDC Health Department
Red Flags Identified:
  • Fake government domain
  • Requests for Social Security Number
  • Artificial urgency
  • Exploits current events (COVID-19)
  • No official CDC branding or contact info
